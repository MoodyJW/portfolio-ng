education

sutff

The first time I wrote code was sometime in the late 90s when I customized my AOL 3.0 profile. After that, I took a break from coding for a while and finished high school, spent four years in the US Army, bartended, sold cars, tutored writing, got a degree in psychology, and worked as tech support for an insurance company.


In early 2019 I decided I wanted to learn Python and become a programmer. I struggled; however, I became obsessed with coding. I'd never understood what it was like to get such a sense of satisfaction from something I could be paid to do.


I quit my insurance job and started coding. Day after day and hour after hour, I beat my brain against the wall that is learning such a complex new skill. Fast-forward a few months and I enrolled in a coding bootcamp. With the help of an instructor and classmates, I started to see serious progress and began learning JavaScript along with Python.


Before the bootcamp ended in November 2019, I had a contract-to-hire position lined up with an oil and gas company in Houston, TX. They wanted me to work on refactoring a massive legacy application into a modern, responsive Angular application. I was ecstatic and terrified, I'd never touched Angular before that interview, which I explained in the interview, yet they still hired me.


After six months, the company offered me a full-time position and I've been here for about two years now. Sometimes I wonder where I'd be if I spent that 20 year break coding instead; nevertheless, I believe my experiences leading up to this point are what provides me with a deep appreciation for being able to do something I love and still pay rent.
